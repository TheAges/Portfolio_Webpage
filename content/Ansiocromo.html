Ansiocromo

what shape does your anxiety have?
Communicative machine and experience - March 2018

Ansiocromo was developed within the Words Works context, an art exhibition of communicative machines based on a specific human mood. Ansiocromo is a "test" for measuring anxiety, which allows us to analyze it and break it down into 4 main sub-categories. In this manner we are able to give to the patient an analog and digital report available online. In order to develop it, we started from the concept "anxiety as a distortion of reality", then we crated the physical machine, the event communication, the experience and the web site for the displaying of the personal patients reports and the global results.

The machine

The fundamental part of the project and the fulcrum of all the experience is ansiocromo machine. It consists of a metal body containing a plastic tray (this one then is filled with a mixture of glue and water that allows the production of the report), a smaller metal frame (where four color tanks and the necessary valves are placed for controlling the color flow) and the operator interface buttons. The electronic heart of the machine is an Arduino.

Coordinated image

The whole experience was accompanied by the appropriate coordinated image, starting from the posters for the event (which were distorted with the help of an analogue method) up to the folder to collect the exam result (distributed to participants), passing through the exam questionnaire and the information brochures, everything was designed in order to following the common thread of "anxiety as distortion of reality".

The website for the exhibition and display
of reports and global results of the experience

The online component linked to the exhibition consists of a website divided in two parts, the first pre-exhibition and the second during and after it. In the first phase, the site had the function of informing about the contents of the exhibition and also presented the concept of distortion, in a digital and interactive way, in the second phase the website instead presents the "digital" form of the report (which can be accessed by framing the appropriate QR code placed on the paper exam). Here specific graphs are shown that allow the patients to read theirs results and allow them to compare their anxiety with those of others. In this phase the visual aspect is more simplified and cleaner to facilitate the reading of the data.
